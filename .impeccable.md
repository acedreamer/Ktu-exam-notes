## Design Context

### Users
KTU B.Tech students preparing for exams. They are often stressed, reading on mobile or desktop late at night, and need to quickly find "Sure Shot" topics and key theory.

### Brand Personality
**Technical Editorial**: Serious, dignified, premium. It feels like a high-end research journal or a well-maintained developer documentation site. Voice is authoritative and helpful.
**3-Word Personality**: Dignified, Utilitarian, Editorial.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Visual Tone**: High contrast, serif-heavy typography (Fraunces/Lora), monochromatic base with strategic accent colors for functional categories. Zero visual noise.
**Theme**: Deep blue-tinted dark mode (default) and warm parchment light mode.

### Design Principles
1. **Content is King**: The notes are the focus. UI should recede until needed.
2. **Functional Color**: Color is never decorative. It indicates section types (Theory vs. Tips) or importance (Sure Shot).
3. **Typographic Hierarchy**: Use distinct fonts to separate UI (Mono), Content (Lora), and Structure (Fraunces).
4. **Fluid Responsiveness**: The reading experience must be flawless on all screen sizes.
5. **Progressive Disclosure**: Sidebar topics only appear for the active module to reduce cognitive load.
